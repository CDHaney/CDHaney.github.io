The following items are links to each of the projects I've done throughout this semester of Writing and Digital Media. These projects were designed to give myself and my classmates experience in different forms and ways of presenting information, along with experience in appealing to a demographic using rhetorical concepts like exigence and audience.

RPGs are for everyone, and you should play one

In this Medium blog post, I attempt to persuade those who are reluctant to play Role Playing Games that they are for a wider demographic than just "nerdy gamers," and that they are worth the time to play.

Senate Over science: why we don't have scientists making technical policies

This second blog post attempts to explain the reason why we don't have scientists making the technical policies in the US government, and why politicians seem to be disconnected from the scientific concepts they are making policies on.

A Comedy of errors: what covid-19 has revealed about the us and its people

This is a podcast I recorded and edited, which features myself and a group of my classmates discussing the US' response to the pandemic, how we feel the nation is handling it, and how the use of comedy to make light of the situation can be helpful or harmful.
