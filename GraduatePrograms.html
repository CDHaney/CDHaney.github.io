Resource Guide by Casey Haney

Graduate Programs

As with any career, a graduate degree will help you excel in the field, by allowing you to work alongside academic professionals and mentors that help you develop professional skills. Of course, some graduate programs will be better than others, whether it be due to the rigor of the coursework, how talented your professors are in that field, or some other criteria. Because of this, it may be difficult to decide what graduate programs to apply for, so below is an overview of the four best graduate programs for software engineers. Keep in mind that for this list, I will be covering which graduate programs provide the best computer science degrees, as that is the most relevant degree for software engineers.

Stanford University

Stanford's computer science graduate program requires the student to choose a specific concentration for study, as opposed to allowing generalized study. These concentrations include a couple that I find particularly interesting, like human-computer interaction and "mobile and internet computing," both of which revolve heavily around the basis of software engineering: design and usage of the software by humans. Besides allowing the student to pick a specific area in computer science to go into, the profesors there to be your guides include top names in the field like Donald Knuth, who wrote The Art of Computer Programming, the widely acclaimed guide to the principles of programming. Overall, the graduate program at Stanford is without a doubt one of the best out there for software engineers.
